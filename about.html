About


 

I’m a software developer / researcher / data scientist based in Munich interested in human-computer interaction, machine learning, functional programming, data visualization and mixed reality.

 

Currently, I’m studying computer science (M.Sc.) at LMU Munich. Previously, I’ve completed my bachelor of Media Informatics at LMU Munich, writing my bachelor thesis at Lancaster University about Interaction in VR.

 

I’ve co-written papers about gaze- and gesture interaction in VR (SUI 2017, IEEE VR 2019) and worked at Intel Mobile Communications, Siemens AG and LMU Munich.

 

Additionally I’ve co-written a popular article featured on instructables.com about building a NFC timeline radio.

 

Presently I’m working as a Research Assistant at the Usable Security and Privacy group of Bundeswehr University in Munich and am responsible for the BehaVR lab, along with supervision on topics ranging from VR/AR to gaze interaction, motion tracking and machine learning.
